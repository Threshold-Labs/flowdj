How it works
Three inputs, one continuous mix
FlowDJ watches how you work and translates your rhythm into evolving music. No playlists. No manual control needed.
01 — Typing

Your keystrokes drive the beat

Every keystroke feeds a flow score that decays when you stop. Type faster and the music builds — energy, tempo, complexity all rise. Pause and it breathes with you. Rapid bursts get a multiplier bonus.

02 — Voice

Your voice builds momentum

Toggle the mic and your vocal energy feeds the same flow score. Thinking out loud, humming, talking through a problem — it all builds momentum. Works in every browser via raw audio analysis.

03 — Focus

Your words reveal your focus

Pick a project with focus keywords. FlowDJ analyzes your recent text in real-time. On-topic writing keeps the music clear and warm. Drift off-topic and the harmony shifts — a subtle dissonance that primes you to refocus.

The positive feedback loop
Music as a mirror for your work state
The insight: continuous music generation forces persistent internal state, temporal continuity, and self-consistency. The same properties you need for deep work. FlowDJ makes that state audible.

When you're in flow, the music locks into a groove that reinforces your momentum. When you drift, the music creates subtle tension — not enough to be annoying, just enough to make coming back feel like resolution. That resolution is rewarding, which keeps you on track.

Spotify integration
Your taste shapes the sound
Connect Spotify and FlowDJ learns what music moves you. Your top genres, artists, and audio features become the DNA of the generative engine.

Every Spotify track has audio features — danceability, valence, energy, acousticness. FlowDJ maps these to synth parameters so the generative music feels like your music, not a generic ambient loop.

With Spotify Premium, FlowDJ can crossfade between generative audio and actual tracks from your library — picking songs that match your current flow state and gradually weaving them in.

Audio feature mapping

danceability→BPM + energy
valence→major/minor key
energy→complexity + drive
acousticness→pad timbre
instrumentalness→lead volume
tempo→base BPM
loudness→master gain curve

Genre → palette

lo-fi / hip-hop→vinyl + Rhodes
electronic→FM + acid bass
jazz→walking bass + brush
ambient→granular + field rec
classical→strings + piano
